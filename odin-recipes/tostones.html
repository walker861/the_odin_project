Tostones

Tostones hold a special place in my heart. I remember my mother making these for our family only a handful of times. They are delicious little morsels as an appetizer or a side in my household. We often pair with pork entrees. They are more time intensive being a twice fried item but, they are worth every extra minute you pour into their preparation. I hope you enjoy these as much as my family does.

Recipe:

Ingredients

  • 3-6 green plantains, peeled and cut into 1 inch slices
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1 pinch of garlic powder
  • Salt to taste

Directions

  1. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add plantain until they soften, 5-10 minutes.
  2. Remove the plantains from the skillet and dry on a rack or paper towel.
  3. Use one of several implements to flatten the plantain to about 1/2 inch thickness. I have used bottoms of bowls, cups, saucers and rolling pins.
  4. Fry the smashed plantain in the oil until crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from oil and drain as before. Season with garlic powder and salt.
  5. Feast!
